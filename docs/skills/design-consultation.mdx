This is **design partner mode**.

`/design-consultation` is for when you have nothing yet — no design system, no font choices, no color palette. You're starting from zero and you want a senior designer to sit down with you and build the whole visual identity together.

## How it works

The agent asks about your product, your users, and your audience. It thinks about what your product needs to communicate — trust, speed, craft, warmth — and works backward to concrete choices. Then it proposes a complete design system:

- **Aesthetic direction** — industrial, editorial, playful, minimal
- **Typography** — 3+ fonts with specific roles (display, body, data)
- **Color palette** — with hex values and rationale
- **Spacing scale** — base unit and density
- **Layout approach** — grid system, breakpoints
- **Motion strategy** — where animation aids comprehension

Every recommendation comes with a rationale. Every choice reinforces every other choice.

## Safe choices AND creative risks

Every dev tool dashboard looks the same — clean sans-serif, muted grays, a blue accent. They're all coherent. They're all forgettable.

`/design-consultation` proposes safe choices AND risks — and tells you which is which:

> "Here are the choices that keep you literate in your category. And here is where I think you should break from convention, and why."

You pick which risks to take. The agent checks that the whole system still coheres either way.

## Landscape research

The agent can research what's already out there in your space — take screenshots of real sites, analyze their fonts, colors, and spacing — so you see the landscape before you make choices.

## Example

```
/design-consultation

Claude: From the README, this looks like a developer analytics dashboard.
        Would you like me to look at what's out there first?

You:    Yes, show me what's out there.

Claude: [browses Linear, Vercel, Datadog, Posthog — takes screenshots]

        Here's the landscape. They all converge on clean sans-serifs
        and muted blue-gray palettes.

        My proposal:
        TYPOGRAPHY: Geist (body), Instrument Serif (display), JetBrains Mono (data)
        COLOR: Slate neutrals, single teal accent (#0D9488)

        SAFE: Geist for body, slate neutrals, grid-disciplined layout
        RISKS: Serif for headings (nobody in dev tools does this),
               teal instead of blue, 4px compact density

You:    Love the serif risk. Let's see it.

Claude: [Opens interactive HTML preview with realistic dashboard mockup]

You:    Ship it.

Claude: Wrote DESIGN.md. Updated CLAUDE.md with design guidelines.
```

## Output

After you agree on the system, it:
1. Generates an interactive HTML preview with realistic product pages in light and dark mode
2. Writes `DESIGN.md` to your repo root
3. Updates `CLAUDE.md` so every future Claude Code session respects the system
